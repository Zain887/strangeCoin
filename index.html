    Tokenomics

    "Taxes 0/0" typically refers to a tax rate of zero percent on transactions within a cryptocurrency ecosystem. This means that no taxes or fees are imposed on transactions, resulting in a tax-free environment for users. This approach is often employed to promote adoption and encourage transactions within the ecosystem by eliminating the financial burden associated with taxes.

    "Supply: 1 million" indicates that the total supply of a cryptocurrency token is one million units. This fixed quantity of tokens is available within the ecosystem and may be distributed among users through various mechanisms such as initial coin offerings (ICOs), airdrops, mining, or other means. The limited supply often contributes to the token's scarcity, potentially impacting its value and demand dynamics within the market.

    "Liquidity burned" is the process of permanently removing tokens from circulation in a cryptocurrency ecosystem. This is typically done to decrease the total token supply, increase scarcity, and potentially boost the value of the remaining tokens. It's a common practice in DeFi projects to manage token economics and encourage value appreciation.

    "Mint & Freeze Revoked" means the cancellation of the ability to create new tokens or restrict token transferability within a cryptocurrency ecosystem. This action is taken to prevent further token creation or restrictions on token movements, often to maintain transparency or decentralization.
